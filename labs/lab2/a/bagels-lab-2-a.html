How to properly perform a sumo-stance deadlift

A sumo stance deadlift is a variation on the traditional straight bar deadlift. While the normal deadlift form involves keeping your stance roughly shoulder width apart,
the sumo deadlift instead requires you stance your legs roughly a foot beyond shoulder width. This results in much greater hip-flexor and quad activation.
Moreover, the variation puts far less pressure on the spine which makes it a great option for those who are worried about back injury.

    The Steps:

  1. Stance yourself about a foot outside shoulder width with the bar placed over the center of your foot.
  2. Perform a hip-hinge in order to bend down and grab the bar (take care to keep the back straight and your core braced)
  3. Grab the bar in either an overhand or switch grip and pull yourself down a little using the bar as leverage.
  4. Take a deep breath and perform the hinge in reverse in order to lift the bar (once again, make sure that the spine is straight).
