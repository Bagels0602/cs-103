Arctic Foxes

Diet

Arctic fox diets usually consist of birds, squirrel, and lemming.


Habitat

The arctic fox is found in treeless coastal areas of Alaska from the Aleutian Islands north to Point Barrow and east to the Canada border. They prefer tundra habitat, usually near rocky shores, and have been observed ranging far out onto pack ice in winter.


Prey and Predators

As can be assumed with their diet, arctic foxes largely prey upon fish and various other forms of small aquatic life. Arctic foxes eat small mammals (especially lemmings), insects, berries, carrion, marine invertebrates, seabirds and fish. Their predators include polar bears, wolves, golden eagles, grizzly bears and humans.

Fun Facts

  1. They change their fur color depending on the season.
  2. A group of Arctic foxes is called a skulk or a leash.
  3. They will often 'snow dive' to hunt for their prey.
  4. Their top speed can go up to 50km/h!
